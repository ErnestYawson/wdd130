GHANA

Ghana is a vibrant West African country known for its rich cultural heritage and diverse landscapes. It was the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence in 1957, led by Kwame Nkrumah. Ghana is a major exporter of cocoa and has significant gold and oil reserves. The country is home to various ethnic groups, including the Akan and Ewe, which contribute to its lively festivals, music, and art. The capital, Accra, blends traditional and modern influences, featuring markets and historical sites. Ghanaian cuisine includes popular dishes like jollof rice and kelewele (spicy fried plantains), and the country is famous for its welcoming people, making it a popular destination for tourists. Its diverse landscapes range from coastal beaches to lush rainforests and national parks, offering numerous ecotourism opportunities.
